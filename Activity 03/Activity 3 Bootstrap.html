Twice (Korean: 트와이스; Japanese: トゥワイス) , commonly stylized as TWICE, is a South Korean girl group formed by JYP Entertainment. The group is composed of nine members: Nayeon, Jeongyeon, Momo, Sana, Jihyo, Mina, Dahyun, Chaeyoung, and Tzuyu. Twice was formed under the television program Sixteen (2015) and debuted on October 20, 2015, with the extended play (EP) The Story Begins.[4] In 2019, they became the best-selling Korean girl group of all-time. During a production presentation for Mnet's new show, "Sixteen" on April 29, Park revealed the new girl group's name as Twice. ... It means that the group will touch people's hearts twice, once through the ears and once again through the eyes.

JAPAN LINE The J-Line of TWICE is a trio composed of members Mina, Sana, and Momo, are colloquially referred to as “Misamo” in Japan. Momo, the oldest Japanese member, was born in Kyoto, Japan in 1996. She attended dance school in Osaka from the age of three. At age 11, she appeared in the music video for LEXY‘s song “Ma People“.
